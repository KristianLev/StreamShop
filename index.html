How StreamShop Works

StreamShop is commerce middleware for streaming platforms. It maps timestamps in live or pre-recorded content to products from official merchant stores. During natural breaks in the content (VCR interludes, pre-encore gaps — never during performances), the overlay surfaces relevant products. Viewers scan a QR code (TV) or tap a button (phone) and complete checkout on the merchant's own store. StreamShop never handles payments.

The problem: Netflix generates massive cultural moments but captures $0 in real-time merchandise revenue. Squid Game merch was 2 weeks late. KPop Demon Hunters merch won't arrive until a year after peak demand. Disney's Eras Tour film had 4.6M views and $0 in-stream merch sales. Amazon proved the model works (Shop the Show across 1,300+ titles, 89% of viewers search for products same day) — but only for products sold on Amazon.com.

Why BTS: On March 21, 2026, BTS performs their first concert in 3+ years, live on Netflix to 300M+ subscribers globally. ARMY (BTS fans) spend $1,422/year on merch and run the most sophisticated buying campaigns in consumer culture — coordinated chart tracking via Hanteo and Circle, country-by-country group orders. The products exist on Weverse Shop. The store exists. There's no purchase path during the stream. StreamShop is the bridge.
